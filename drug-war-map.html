Sources

The mention of external data sources or research papers does not mean an endorsement of this website. The contents of this website are solely the responsibility of its author.

  • Drug War-Related Homicides: Dec 2006-Dec 2010: Base de datos de fallecimientos ocurridos por presunta rivalidad delincuencial and for 2011 PGR
  • Total Homicides: Dirección General de Información en Salud (DGIS). Base de datos de defunciones 1998-2010: Sistema Nacional de Información en Salud (SINAIS). [México]: Secretaría de Salud [Accessed 2011-01-20] INEGI
  • Military Operation Dates: Segundo Informe de Labores, Secretaría de Seguridad Pública
  • Coordinates of Municipalities: Catálogos de municipios y localidades
  • Metro Areas: Delimitación de las zonas metropolitanas de México 2005, INEGI
  • Population estimates based on historical data by CONAPO/COLMEX and the 2010 Census: Source
  • Drug lab location data from various newspapers: Spreadsheet
  • Mariguana and opium poppy eradication data from: El mapa del cultivo de drogas en México, by Carlos Resa Nestares
  • Best routes to reach the U.S. border from the municipalities with the highest drug plant eradication and drug lab density made with Google Directions
  • For the importance of trafficking networks read: Trafficking Networks and the Mexican Drug War by Melissa Dell
  • For the importance of cartel leader arrests read: Security, drugs, and violence in mexico: a survey, by Eduardo Guerrero-Gutiérrez
  • For the importance of the expiration of the assault weapon ban read: Cross-border spillover: US Gun Law and violence in Mexico by Dube, A., Dube, O. and Garcia-Ponce, O. and my blog post
